Frequently Asked Questions

Get answers to the most common questions about final expense insurance

What is final expense insurance?

+

Final expense insurance, also known as burial insurance, is a type of whole life insurance specifically designed to cover end-of-life costs such as funeral expenses, burial costs, medical bills, and other final expenses. These policies typically range from $5,000 to $50,000 and are easier to qualify for than traditional life insurance.

How much does final expense insurance cost?

+

Final expense insurance premiums typically range from $15 to $100+ per month, depending on your age, gender, health, coverage amount, and tobacco use. For example, a healthy 65-year-old non-smoker might pay around $35-45 per month for $15,000 in coverage. Our assessment tool provides personalized estimates based on your specific situation.

Do I need a medical exam?

+

Most final expense insurance policies do not require a medical exam. Instead, you'll answer a few simple health questions on the application. Some policies even offer guaranteed acceptance with no health questions at all, though these may have a waiting period before full benefits are available.

What does final expense insurance cover?

+

Final expense insurance can cover funeral and burial costs, cremation expenses, outstanding medical bills, credit card debt, legal fees, and any other final expenses. The death benefit is paid directly to your beneficiaries, who can use the money for any purpose they choose.

How quickly can I get coverage?

+

Many final expense policies offer same-day approval and coverage that begins immediately upon approval and payment of the first premium. Some policies with guaranteed acceptance may have a waiting period (typically 2-3 years) before full death benefits are available.

Can I be denied coverage?

+

With simplified issue policies, you may be declined based on health questions, but approval rates are generally high. Guaranteed issue policies accept everyone regardless of health, but typically have lower coverage amounts and waiting periods. Age limits usually range from 45-85 years old.

What's the difference between final expense and term life insurance?

+

Final expense insurance is permanent whole life coverage with smaller benefit amounts ($5,000-$50,000) and easier qualification. Term life insurance offers larger benefits but requires medical exams, has temporary coverage periods, and becomes very expensive as you age. Final expense premiums never increase and coverage never expires.

How much coverage do I need?

+

The average funeral costs between $7,000-$12,000, but total final expenses can reach $15,000-$25,000 when including burial plots, headstones, flowers, and other costs. Consider your outstanding debts, final medical bills, and what you want to leave for your family. Our assessment tool helps determine the right amount for your situation.
